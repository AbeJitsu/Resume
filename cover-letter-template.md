## Customization Guide

### Before Each Application:

1. **Research the Company** (10 minutes)
   - Visit their website, GitHub, engineering blog
   - Understand their product and mission
   - Identify tech stack and team structure if available

2. **Match Job Requirements** (10 minutes)
   - Extract 3-5 key requirements from job posting
   - Identify which of your accomplishments address these
   - Note any skills they emphasize multiple times

3. **Personalize Opening** (5 minutes)
   - Address hiring manager by name if possible (check LinkedIn)
   - Mention specific project or company detail showing you've done research
   - Connect their mission to why you're interested

4. **Customize Body Paragraphs** (15 minutes)
   - **Paragraph 1:** Choose or blend the role-specific opening based on position type
   - **Paragraph 2:** Replace bracketed sections with your matched accomplishments
   - Use metrics from source documents (60-80%, 15-50x, 431+ commits, 534+ entries, etc.)

5. **Final Polish** (5 minutes)
   - Proofread for spelling/grammar
   - Ensure tone matches company culture (startup vs. enterprise, formal vs. conversational)
   - Keep to 3-4 paragraphs (one-page maximum)
   - Verify all [brackets] are filled with specific information

### Tone Guidance

- **Startup/Fast-Growing Company:** Conversational, enthusiastic, emphasize impact and shipping speed
- **Enterprise/Large Company:** Professional, structured, emphasize systems thinking and quality
- **Early-Stage/Startup:** Show entrepreneurial thinking, reference your own company experience
- **Established Company:** Emphasize process, testing, and long-term maintainability

### What NOT to Include

❌ Generic statements ("I'm a hard worker," "I'm passionate about technology")
❌ Redundancy with resume (don't just repeat your experience)
❌ Negative comments about previous employers
❌ Over-explanation of career pivots (let your accomplishments speak)
❌ Technical jargon without context (explain why it matters)

### What TO Include

✅ Specific metrics and quantifiable results
✅ Evidence that you researched the company
✅ Connection between your skills and their stated needs
✅ Concrete examples from your work
✅ Professional tone appropriate to their culture
✅ Clear enthusiasm for the specific role and company
